Foundations of Computer Science is a course which focuses on mathematical proofs,
functions, relations, set theory, recusion and functional programming, and logic.
  • The course started off with propositional logic, simple truth tables, logcal equivalences, and symbolic proofs.
  • From here, we began converting propositional logic into the equivalent digital circuits and simplifying logical expressions.
  • The course began discussion formal proofs, number theory, and different proof techniques such as
    • Mathematical Induction
    • Contradiction
    • Contrapositon
  • After proofs, we were introduced to Set Theory, Set Indentities and algebraic proofs involving Sets, along with complex boolean algebra.
  • Near the end of the course, we discussed Functions, Composition of Functions, Functions operating on Sets, and basic properties of Functions.
  • Following Functions, we then went on to discuss Recusive sequences, Recursively defined Sets, as well as the Recursive language of SML.
    Our homeworks consisted of implementing recursive sorts, performing operations on lists, as well as implementing goldbach's conjecture recursively.
  • Finally, the course ended with Relations, funamental properties such as Reflexivity, Symmetry, and Transitivity, Equivalence classes, Antisymmetry and Topological Sorting.
